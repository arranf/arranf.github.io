I'm Arran France and I'm a Software Engineering student at Royal Holloway University.

I build things in Java, C#, Python, Rust and JavaScript for mobile, web, and desktop. Some interesting projects of mine include a static client-side encryption web service for Travis CI, a JavaFX implementation of Conway's Game of Life, and LowScore an Android app for IsThereAnyDeal.com.

In the Summer of 2016, I took part in an undergraduate research project at Royal Holloway during which I produced an Angular.JS based web frontend and REST service for ExpoSE.JS, a tool for JavaScript symbolic execution. I also introduced a mechanism for pluggable serialization of ExpoSE's output.

Following my undergraduate research project I did a five week internship at the Spark Development Network working on v6 of Rock, including a system for notifications.

In 2016 I was awarded The Ede & Ravenscroft First Year Science Faculty Prize for the best academic performance by a first year in the Faculty of Science, the Driver Prize for the best first year departmental performance, and a letter of commendation for my academic achievement.

When I'm not studying I run Bricks and Mortar Studio, a development studio that produces plugins and provides custom development and consultancy for Rock RMS. When I'm relaxing you can normally find me playing Arena Gods, writing, fishing, running, or practising bass guitar.
